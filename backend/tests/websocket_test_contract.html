Article 2: Recitals

WHEREAS, the Parties wish to explore a potential business relationship, collaboration, partnership, or transaction (the "Purpose") that may involve the exchange of certain confidential, proprietary, and/or trade secret information;

WHEREAS, in connection with the Purpose, each Party may disclose to the other Party certain Confidential Information (as defined herein) that is valuable, proprietary, and confidential in nature;

WHEREAS, each Party acknowledges that the Confidential Information to be disclosed is of substantial value to the Disclosing Party and has been developed through the expenditure of substantial time, effort, and money;

WHEREAS, each Party recognizes that unauthorized disclosure or use of the Confidential Information could cause irreparable harm and significant injury to the Disclosing Party, the extent of which may be difficult to ascertain;

WHEREAS, the Parties desire to protect the confidential and proprietary nature of any Confidential Information disclosed under this Agreement;

WHEREAS, each Party is willing to disclose Confidential Information to the other Party only upon the terms and conditions set forth in this Agreement;

Article 3: Definitions

For purposes of this Agreement, the following terms shall have the meanings set forth below:

3.1 "Confidential Information"

The term "Confidential Information" means any and all information, data, materials, documents, or knowledge, whether written, oral, electronic, visual, or in any other form or medium, that is disclosed by or on behalf of one Party (the "Disclosing Party") to the other Party (the "Receiving Party"), whether before, on, or after the Effective Date of this Agreement, including but not limited to:

  • Technical Information: Research, development, designs, specifications, drawings, blueprints, diagrams, models, prototypes, samples, formulas, algorithms, processes, methods, techniques, know-how, inventions (whether patentable or not), improvements, discoveries, technical data, and engineering information;
  • Business Information: Business plans, strategies, forecasts, budgets, projections, financial information, financial statements, pricing information, cost data, profit margins, sales data, customer lists, customer information, customer preferences, supplier lists, supplier information, distribution methods, marketing plans, marketing strategies, advertising campaigns, and market research;
  • Product Information: Product plans, product designs, product specifications, product development information, product roadmaps, manufacturing processes, quality control procedures, and testing procedures;
  • Personnel Information: Employee information, compensation data, organizational structures, personnel policies, training materials, and human resources information;
  • Intellectual Property: Trade secrets, copyrights, trademarks, patents, patent applications, proprietary rights, and any other intellectual property or proprietary information;
  • Computer Information: Software, source code, object code, databases, data compilations, computer programs, systems, applications, user interfaces, and any related documentation;
  • Legal and Contractual Information: Contracts, agreements, terms and conditions, legal opinions, regulatory filings, compliance information, and correspondence with legal counsel;
  • Strategic Information: Merger and acquisition information, joint venture information, partnership information, strategic alliance information, and investment information;
  • Any other information that is marked, designated, or identified as "Confidential," "Proprietary," or with a similar legend at the time of disclosure, or that would reasonably be considered confidential or proprietary under the circumstances of disclosure.

3.2 "Affiliate"

The term "Affiliate" means, with respect to any Party, any entity that directly or indirectly controls, is controlled by, or is under common control with such Party. For purposes of this definition, "control" means the possession, directly or indirectly, of the power to direct or cause the direction of the management and policies of an entity, whether through ownership of voting securities, by contract, or otherwise.

3.3 "Representatives"

The term "Representatives" means, with respect to any Party, its Affiliates, and its and their respective directors, officers, employees, agents, consultants, advisors (including financial advisors, legal counsel, and accountants), and other representatives who have a legitimate need to know the Confidential Information for purposes of evaluating or pursuing the Purpose.

3.4 "Purpose"

The term "Purpose" means the evaluation, consideration, negotiation, and/or implementation of a potential business relationship, transaction, collaboration, partnership, or other arrangement between the Parties, as may be more specifically described by the Parties in writing from time to time.

3.5 "Effective Date"

The term "Effective Date" means the date first written above, being the date on which this Agreement becomes effective and binding upon the Parties.

3.6 "Agreement"

The term "Agreement" means this Mutual Non-Disclosure Agreement, including all schedules, exhibits, and attachments hereto, as may be amended from time to time in accordance with the terms hereof.

Article 4: Scope and Disclosure of Confidential Information

4.1 Disclosure Authorization

Subject to the terms and conditions of this Agreement, each Party may disclose its Confidential Information to the other Party solely for the Purpose. The Disclosing Party retains all rights, title, and interest in and to its Confidential Information. No license or other rights in or to any Confidential Information are granted under this Agreement, except as expressly set forth herein.

4.2 Form of Disclosure

Confidential Information may be disclosed in written, oral, electronic, visual, or any other form or medium. When Confidential Information is disclosed in tangible form, the Disclosing Party shall mark or label such information as "Confidential," "Proprietary," or with a similar legend. When Confidential Information is disclosed orally or visually, the Disclosing Party shall identify such information as confidential at the time of disclosure and shall provide written confirmation of such confidential disclosure to the Receiving Party within thirty (30) days after such oral or visual disclosure, summarizing the Confidential Information disclosed.

4.3 No Obligation to Disclose

Nothing in this Agreement shall obligate either Party to disclose any particular Confidential Information to the other Party. Each Party shall determine, in its sole discretion, what Confidential Information, if any, it wishes to disclose to the other Party.

4.4 Accuracy of Information

While each Party shall endeavor to provide accurate Confidential Information, neither Party makes any representation or warranty, express or implied, as to the accuracy or completeness of its Confidential Information. Neither Party shall have any liability to the other Party or to any other person resulting from the use of or reliance upon the Confidential Information disclosed hereunder.

4.5 Method of Disclosure

Confidential Information may be disclosed through any reasonable means, including but not limited to in-person meetings, telephone conversations, video conferences, electronic mail, file sharing platforms, physical delivery of documents, or any other method agreed upon by the Parties. The Receiving Party acknowledges that it shall be responsible for maintaining the confidentiality of Confidential Information regardless of the method by which such information is disclosed.

Article 5: Obligations of Receiving Party

5.1 Duty of Confidentiality

The Receiving Party agrees to hold and maintain the Disclosing Party's Confidential Information in strict confidence and to protect the Confidential Information from unauthorized disclosure or use. The Receiving Party shall use at least the same degree of care to protect the Confidential Information as it uses to protect its own confidential information of a similar nature, but in no event less than reasonable care.

5.2 Limited Use

The Receiving Party agrees to use the Confidential Information solely for the Purpose and for no other purpose whatsoever. The Receiving Party shall not use the Confidential Information for its own benefit or for the benefit of any third party, except as expressly authorized in writing by the Disclosing Party.

5.3 Limited Disclosure

The Receiving Party shall not disclose any Confidential Information to any person or entity except to its Representatives who:

  1. Have a legitimate need to know such Confidential Information for purposes of evaluating or pursuing the Purpose;
  2. Have been informed by the Receiving Party of the confidential nature of the Confidential Information; and
  3. Have agreed to be bound by confidentiality obligations at least as restrictive as those contained in this Agreement, or are otherwise bound by professional obligations of confidentiality.

The Receiving Party shall be responsible for any breach of this Agreement by any of its Representatives and shall take all reasonable measures to restrain its Representatives from unauthorized disclosure or use of the Confidential Information.

5.4 Protection Measures

The Receiving Party agrees to implement and maintain appropriate administrative, technical, and physical safeguards to protect the Confidential Information from unauthorized access, use, disclosure, modification, or destruction. Such safeguards shall include, but not be limited to:

  • Restricting access to Confidential Information to only those Representatives who need to know such information for the Purpose;
  • Maintaining secure storage facilities and systems for Confidential Information;
  • Implementing appropriate password protection and encryption for electronic Confidential Information;
  • Establishing and enforcing policies and procedures for handling Confidential Information;
  • Training Representatives on their obligations with respect to Confidential Information;
  • Monitoring compliance with this Agreement and taking corrective action when necessary.

5.5 No Reverse Engineering

The Receiving Party shall not reverse engineer, disassemble, decompile, or otherwise attempt to derive the source code, underlying ideas, algorithms, structure, or organization of any Confidential Information that constitutes software, technology, or technical information, except to the extent that such restriction is expressly prohibited by applicable law.

5.6 No Copying or Modification

The Receiving Party shall not copy, reproduce, modify, adapt, translate, or create derivative works based upon the Confidential Information without the prior written consent of the Disclosing Party, except to the extent reasonably necessary for the Purpose.

5.7 Notification of Unauthorized Disclosure

The Receiving Party shall promptly notify the Disclosing Party in writing upon discovery of any unauthorized disclosure, use, or loss of Confidential Information, or any breach or threatened breach of this Agreement. The Receiving Party shall cooperate with the Disclosing Party in every reasonable way to help the Disclosing Party regain possession of the Confidential Information and prevent its further unauthorized use or disclosure.

Article 6: Exceptions to Confidentiality Obligations

The obligations set forth in Article 5 shall not apply to any Confidential Information that the Receiving Party can demonstrate by clear and convincing evidence:

6.1 Public Domain

Was in the public domain at the time of disclosure or thereafter becomes part of the public domain through no breach of this Agreement by the Receiving Party or its Representatives;

6.2 Prior Knowledge

Was known to the Receiving Party prior to disclosure by the Disclosing Party, as evidenced by written records prepared prior to such disclosure;

6.3 Independent Development

Was independently developed by the Receiving Party without reference to or use of the Confidential Information, as evidenced by written records;

6.4 Rightful Receipt from Third Party

Was rightfully received by the Receiving Party from a third party without breach of any confidentiality obligation and without restriction on disclosure or use;

6.5 Authorized Disclosure

Was approved for release by prior written authorization of the Disclosing Party;

6.6 Required Disclosure

Is required to be disclosed by law, regulation, court order, subpoena, or other legal process; provided, however, that the Receiving Party shall:

  1. Promptly notify the Disclosing Party in writing of such requirement prior to disclosure (unless prohibited by law or legal process);
  2. Cooperate with the Disclosing Party, at the Disclosing Party's expense, in any effort by the Disclosing Party to seek a protective order or other appropriate remedy;
  3. Disclose only that portion of the Confidential Information that is legally required to be disclosed; and
  4. Use reasonable efforts to obtain assurances that confidential treatment will be accorded to the disclosed Confidential Information.

The Receiving Party acknowledges that these exceptions are to be narrowly construed and that the burden of proving that any Confidential Information falls within one of these exceptions rests solely with the Receiving Party.

Article 7: Term and Termination

7.1 Term

This Agreement shall commence on the Effective Date and shall continue in full force and effect for a period of five (5) years from the Effective Date (the "Term"), unless earlier terminated in accordance with the provisions of this Article 7.

7.2 Termination

Either Party may terminate this Agreement at any time, with or without cause, by providing thirty (30) days' prior written notice to the other Party. Notwithstanding such termination, the obligations of the Receiving Party with respect to Confidential Information disclosed prior to the effective date of termination shall survive for a period of five (5) years from the date of such disclosure, or for such longer period as may be required to protect trade secrets under applicable law.

7.3 Return or Destruction of Confidential Information

Upon termination of this Agreement, or at any time upon the written request of the Disclosing Party, the Receiving Party shall, at the Disclosing Party's option:

  1. Promptly return to the Disclosing Party all documents, materials, and other tangible items containing or representing Confidential Information, including all copies, reproductions, summaries, analyses, or extracts thereof or based thereon; or
  2. Destroy all such documents, materials, and items and certify such destruction in writing to the Disclosing Party.

Notwithstanding the foregoing, the Receiving Party may retain one (1) copy of the Confidential Information in its legal files solely for the purpose of monitoring its continuing obligations under this Agreement. Any such retained Confidential Information shall remain subject to the confidentiality obligations set forth in this Agreement.

7.4 Electronic Records

With respect to Confidential Information stored in electronic form, the Receiving Party shall use commercially reasonable efforts to delete or destroy such information from its systems and shall certify such deletion or destruction in writing to the Disclosing Party. The Receiving Party acknowledges that Confidential Information may remain in archived or backup systems in the ordinary course of the Receiving Party's information management practices, and such residual Confidential Information shall remain subject to the confidentiality obligations of this Agreement.
